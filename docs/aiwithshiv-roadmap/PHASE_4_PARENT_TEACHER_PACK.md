# Phase 4 Parent and Teacher Pack

## Purpose

Provide launch-ready parent and teacher copy for AIWithShiv v1. This content explains safety, learning value, classroom use, privacy, and certificates without adding child login or collecting child private data.

## Parent Intro Copy

### Short Version

AIWithShiv helps children aged 5-10 learn basic AI through comic stories, tiny quizzes, games, and Shiv, a friendly robot-superhero tutor. Core learning works without a child login. Lessons are bundled in the app, and progress is stored locally on the device.

### Screen Copy

Title: What your child learns with Shiv

Body:

AIWithShiv introduces AI in a playful, safe, and age-appropriate way. Children learn through short missions using familiar examples like tiffin, mangoes, cricket, classroom activities, Diwali lights, rangoli, friends, and family.

Your child learns:

- What AI is in simple words.
- Where AI appears at home and school.
- How patterns and examples help AI learn.
- Why AI can make mistakes.
- How to ask better questions.
- Why passwords, phone numbers, addresses, school IDs, and private photos should stay secret.

Parent trust points:

- No child login is required for core learning.
- No phone number, email, password, or address is needed from the child.
- Core progress is stored locally on the device.
- Core lessons and games can work offline.
- ShivBot should use a separate backend only when enabled.
- Ads are disabled by default unless a compliant child-safe ad setup is intentionally enabled.

Closing line:

AIWithShiv is designed to make your child curious, confident, and careful with AI.

## Parent FAQ

### Does my child need an account?

No. The core learning app is local-first and does not require a child account.

### Is the app only for Android and iOS?

Yes. The current launch direction is mobile-first for Android and iOS.

### Does the app need internet?

The core lessons, quizzes, games, rewards, and progress work locally. ShivBot may need internet only if AI chat is enabled through a separate backend.

### What data is stored?

The core app stores learning progress on the device, such as XP, coins, completed lessons, game progress, badges, profile preferences, and settings.

### What should parents discuss after lessons?

Ask simple questions:

- What did Shiv teach today?
- Was the AI answer always correct?
- What private information should we never share?
- Can you show me one pattern or example from the lesson?

## Teacher One-Page Guide: AI Masti Missions

### Course Objective

Students understand AI as a helpful computer system that can use examples, answer questions, and make mistakes. They also learn basic privacy safety.

### Best Fit

- Ages: 5-10
- Format: Individual app learning, small group discussion, or classroom projection
- Time: 10 lessons, 5-8 minutes each

### Key Vocabulary

- AI
- Example
- Helper
- Question
- Mistake
- Privacy
- Trusted adult

### Classroom Activity

AI Helper Hunt:

Ask students to name helpful machines or apps they see at home or school. Examples can include voice assistants, maps, video recommendations, smart toys, or learning apps. Then ask: "Can this helper be wrong sometimes?"

### Discussion Questions

- What is AI in simple words?
- Where have you seen AI-like helpers?
- Why should we check important answers?
- What information should stay private?

### Assessment

Students can pass the course if they can say:

- AI is a computer helper.
- AI can learn from examples.
- AI can make mistakes.
- Private information should not be shared.

### Safety Note

Use calm language. Do not scare children. Say: "Some information is special for family and trusted adults only."

## Teacher One-Page Guide: Pattern Playground

### Course Objective

Students identify patterns, odd items, ordered clues, and simple predictions using familiar Indian examples.

### Best Fit

- Ages: 5-10
- Format: Individual learning, group games, board activity, or worksheet
- Time: 10 lessons, 5-8 minutes each

### Key Vocabulary

- Pattern
- Repeat
- Clue
- Prediction
- Odd one out
- Evidence

### Classroom Activity

Rangoli Pattern Circle:

Draw or show a simple color pattern like red, yellow, red, yellow. Ask students what comes next. Then change to shape or sound patterns. Connect it to AI by saying: "AI also looks for patterns, but it still needs checking."

### Discussion Questions

- What pattern did you find?
- What comes next?
- Which item does not fit?
- How can we check if our guess is correct?

### Assessment

Students can pass the course if they can:

- Continue a simple pattern.
- Find an odd item.
- Make a simple prediction.
- Explain that a smart guess should be checked.

### Safety Note

Patterns help us guess, but guesses are not always truth. Encourage children to check with a teacher, book, parent, or trusted source.

## Teacher One-Page Guide: Teach The Robot

### Course Objective

Students understand that AI can learn from examples, categories, good data, correction, and feedback.

### Best Fit

- Ages: 5-10
- Format: Individual app learning, sorting activity, group classification game
- Time: 10 lessons, 5-8 minutes each

### Key Vocabulary

- Example
- Category
- Data
- Label
- Training
- Feedback
- Mistake

### Classroom Activity

Train ShivBot Sorting Game:

Give students cards with animals, fruits, vehicles, and school objects. Ask them to sort the cards into groups. Then intentionally place one wrong card and ask: "If ShivBot learns from this wrong example, what might happen?"

### Discussion Questions

- What is an example?
- What is a category?
- Why does good data matter?
- How can feedback help a robot learn better?

### Assessment

Students can pass the course if they can:

- Sort examples into categories.
- Explain that wrong examples can teach wrong answers.
- Explain that feedback can help AI improve.
- Give one safe example of training a robot.

### Safety Note

Children should understand that humans guide AI. AI should not be treated as always correct.

## Parent Privacy Explanation

AIWithShiv v1 is designed as a local-first child learning app.

Plain-language version:

Your child can use the core learning app without creating an account. The app does not need your child's phone number, email, password, address, school ID, or private photos for core learning. Lesson content is bundled inside the app, and learning progress is saved on the device.

What may be stored locally:

- Completed lessons.
- Quiz progress.
- Game progress.
- XP, coins, badges, and level.
- Simple profile preferences like avatar and sound settings.

What is not needed for core learning:

- Child email.
- Child phone number.
- Child address.
- Child password.
- School ID.
- Private photos.
- Social profile.

Important note:

If ShivBot AI chat is enabled, it should connect through a separate backend that protects the API key and filters unsafe content. The Flutter app must not contain direct AI API keys.
